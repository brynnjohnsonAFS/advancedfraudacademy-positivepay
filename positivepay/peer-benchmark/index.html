Page 4 | Fee structures & buyer criteria
Fee structures

Four ways community FIs charge for Positive Pay.

Pulled from the ICBA Modern Positive Pay webinar. Most FIs use one or two of these. Some layer all four over time.

01

Per item or per exception

A flat monthly fee plus a small per-item charge ($0.05 to $0.15). Common at FIs that publish full Treasury Management fee schedules.

Good when business clients run high volumes and want the line item visible.
02

Bundled into a higher business account tier

Positive Pay is included in the premium business checking package. The fee shows up as the tier uplift, not as a separate Positive Pay line.

Good when relationship managers want to upgrade tier mix without selling a feature.
03

Relationship pricing

Premium-tier or longer-tenure clients get a discounted or waived rate. Newer or smaller relationships pay full price.

Good when treasury wants Positive Pay as a retention lever, not a revenue line.
04

Mandate after a fraud event

Required enrollment for any business client that has taken a check or ACH loss. Often paired with a disclosure update.

Good when fraud loss history is concentrated in a known segment of accounts.
Buyer criteria

The seven things to look for

in a Modern Positive Pay vendor.
  1. Ready to launch. Fast configuration, streamlined exception workflow, no 6-month implementation.
  2. Flexibility. Customizable rules, ACH filters, exception lists per business client.
  3. AI-powered payee match. Reads handwriting nuance. Cuts false positives.
  4. Real connections. Core, digital banking, accounting, treasury systems integrated, not bolted on.
  5. Modern UI. Looks like the rest of your branch transformation, not a 2009 web portal.
  6. Mobile and SMS decisioning. Business owners decision exceptions from a phone, not a desktop.
  7. File-format agnostic. Accepts whatever the business's accounting system exports.
